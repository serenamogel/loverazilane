    Is normality and molarity the same?

    Main Difference – Molarity vs Normality

    The main difference between molarity and normality is that molarity is the number of moles of a compound present in a mixture of compounds whereas normality is the amount of gram equivalents of a compound present in a mixture of compounds.

    How do I calculate molarity?

    To calculate the molarity of a solution, you divide the moles of solute by the volume of the solution expressed in liters. Note that the volume is in liters of solution and not liters of solvent. When a molarity is reported, the unit is the symbol M and is read as “molar”.
